Midpoint formula

The midpoint between two points $(x_1,y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$ is $(x,y)$ , then

$(x,y)=\left(\dfrac{x_1+x_2}{2},\dfrac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right) $

Distance formula

The distance between two points $(x_1,y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$ is $(x,y)$ , then

$\text{distance}=\sqrt{x_2-x_1+y_2-y_1}$


Example 1

 If the coordinate of the two points $P$ and $Q$ are $(3,-4)$ and $(5,4)$ respectively. Find :

(a) the midpoint of $PQ$.

(b) the length of segment $PQ$.

Solution

$P=(3,-4),Q=(5,4)$

(a) midpoint of $PQ$

$\begin{aligned} & =\left(\dfrac{3+5}{2},\dfrac{-4+4}{2}\right)\\\\ & =(4,0)\end{aligned}$

(b)length of $ PQ$

$\begin{aligned} &=\sqrt{(5-3)^2+(4+4)^2}\\\\ &=\sqrt{4+64}\\\\ &=\sqrt{68} \end{aligned}$
